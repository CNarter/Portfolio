Worthing Street Game - a plan!

As part of a recent Project Management Bootcamp, I was asked to design a project, and I came up with creating a Worthing Street Game. The idea is a fun, interactive street game, playable on any mobile device, showcasing interesting historical and architectural places in Worthing. The project would create an enjoyable, interactive, multiplayer game playable over a small, accessible, mapped area in central Worthing. It would include 10-15 interesting clues to solve, covering facts, views, architectural, or historical places that are always visible and not dependent on seasons, weather, or regular changes/closures. The 'journey' would be on foot, and I took care in planning to ensure it's accessible. I thought about possible monetization via sponsorship or pay-per-play, and considered that in the project and build design

Key Features:

  • Interactive map with a flat, accessible route, including the museum, pier, and cinema, with no steps and with safer road crossing spaces.
  • Location-based puzzles and clues designed around permanent landmarks in Worthing.
  • Future potential for 'side quest' add-ons and monetization via sponsorship or pay-per-play.
  • Designed to be inclusive and accessible, including features for disabled access and 'changing places' toilets.

Skills demonstrated

This bootcamp was a great exercise, refreshing and renewing skills I've built over my career (and building my confidence in my existing knowledge), and adding new aspects of project management that I hadn't previously had exposure to:

  • Project design, scoping, and setup.
  • Stakeholder management and engagement.
  • Risk management and mitigation planning.
  • Strategic planning and SWOT analysis.
  • Content creation, including writing clues and photographing locations.
  • Monitoring and evaluation planning, including setting up analytics and feedback mechanisms.
  • Agile project management methodologies.
  • Collaboration and teamwork in a bootcamp environment.

The design includes:

  • A detailed project plan outlining the scope, objectives, and deliverables.
  • A risk management plan to identify potential challenges and mitigation strategies.
  • A stakeholder engagement plan to ensure community involvement and support.
  • A content creation strategy for developing clues and puzzles.
  • A monitoring and evaluation framework to assess the project's success and impact.
